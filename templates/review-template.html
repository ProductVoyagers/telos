3/5

+ What serves the KRs

The streak banner ("5-day streak") is the first thing the user sees. Putting the thing the user has to lose at the very top leans on loss aversion — the strongest lever a habit app has for daily return. People come back to protect a streak more reliably than to chase a new reward. Today
Retention Engagement
One-tap check-off keeps the cost of a daily visit near zero. Each habit completes with a single tap, no confirmation step. The lower the effort per check-in, the more likely the behavior survives a busy day — which is exactly what a daily-retention habit needs. Today
Engagement

~ What to reconsider

1
Nothing on the screen gives the user a reason to come back tomorrow. The screen shows today's state, but once habits are checked off there's no forward hook — no "your streak continues tomorrow," no preview of tomorrow's plan. Retention is a tomorrow problem, and the screen only speaks to today. Today - after all habits checked
HIGH IMPACT Retention
2
A single missed day resets the streak to zero with nothing to soften the loss. The streak is the only progress signal, which makes it brittle. The biggest retention cliff in habit apps is the day after the first miss — and with no streak-freeze or "6 of the last 7 days" framing, one slip reads as total failure. Today - streak banner
HIGH IMPACT Retention
3
The streak is the only measure of progress — no weekly or longer-horizon signal. "5-day streak" and "2 / 4 done" both describe a single point in time. A second, more forgiving weekly signal would let a user who broke a streak still feel they're winning — which is the KR 2 behavior the app wants to grow. Today - streak banner
MEDIUM IMPACT Engagement
4
No reminder or notification opt-in appears anywhere in the flow. Daily return is almost entirely driven by a timely reminder, yet the screen never asks for notification permission or lets the user set a habit time. Without it, retention depends on the user remembering to open the app unprompted — the weakest possible mechanism. Flow-level
HIGH IMPACT Retention
5
The late-day "still incomplete" state isn't designed for. At "2 / 4 done" the screen looks identical at 9am and 9pm. There's no escalation as the day runs out and the streak is at risk — no "2 habits left to keep your streak." The moment a streak is in danger is the moment a nudge matters most, and it passes silently. Today - partial completion

* Suggestions tied to the KRs

6
Ask for notification permission during onboarding, tied to the user's chosen habit time. When the user adds their first habit, let them pick a time and request notifications in the same step ("We'll remind you at 8am"). A reminder set to when the habit actually happens is the strongest driver of next-day return.
Retention Activation
7
Add a streak-freeze / grace day so one miss doesn't reset progress to zero. Give users a few "freezes" that protect a streak on a missed day. Softening the day-after-a-miss cliff keeps users in the loop long enough to rebuild the habit, directly protecting 7-day retention.
Retention
8
Show a weekly progress signal ("5 of 7 days") alongside the streak. A forgiving, week-level measure gives users who slipped something they're still winning at, and makes "weekly active streaks" (KR 2) a visible goal. Pair it with the streak rather than replacing it.
Engagement Retention
9
Add an end-of-day nudge state: "2 habits left to keep your streak." When habits remain unfinished and the day is running out, escalate the screen (and a notification) to make the streak-at-risk explicit. This converts the highest-intent moment — streak in danger — into a completed check-in.
